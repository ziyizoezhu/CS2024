Botox

What is Botox?

Botulinum toxin, or more commonly known as botox, is often used to reduce wrinkles and creases on skin. It is a neurotoxic protein that is injected into muscles in order to induce muscle weakness or prevent muscle contraction. This helps facial muscles to relax and improves the appearance of wrinkles. This also helps to reduce the size of prominent muscles in some areas of the face, for example, the jaw. Excessive sweating can also be treated using botox.

Why it’s done?

As we age, our skin collagen and underlying soft tissue diminish with time resulting in more wrinkles on our face. Eventually, the wrinkles that initially only appear when you smile or raise your eyebrow, become deeper and more prominent even when we are not trying to express ourselves.

How does the treatment work?

During the procedure, a numbing cream may be applied and botulinum toxin is then injected into the particular area where facial muscle reside. This block muscle nerve signals, which then weakens the muscle so it cannot contract as much as before. The best part is that it requires minimal downtime, meaning you can resume your daily activities without worries.

Popular areas treated with Botox

  • Frown lines
  • Forehead wrinkles
  • Crow’s feet
  • Jaw Reduction (V shaped face)
  • Gummy Smile
  • Bunny Lines
  • Shoulder Slimming
  • Hyperhidrosis (excessive sweating)
  • Calf Slimming
  • Eyebrow lift
  • Cobblestoned chin

Each session should take approximately 25 to 40 minutes, depending on the area of treatment and dosage required

The procedure will consist of:
• 15 minutes consultation (for new patients)
• 15 minutes of numbing and preparation of the facial area
• And 10 to 15 minutes for the primary Botox treatment.
After your treatment, you will see early results within 3 to 4 days. And after 10 to 14 days, the full effect of the procedure will finally take place!

A typical Botox could last 4 to 6 months depending on the area of injection, dosage, your existing facial muscle activity and skin condition.

Botox treatments are not permanent, which means that patients do not need to commit to a lifetime of change.

You may also opt to get Botox treatments as often as once every two to three months to maintain your treatments’ efficacy.

The injections are quick with minimal to no pain at all. It is a tiny injection, and most people can barely feel it.

As we numb the treatment area prior to the injection, there will be minimal discomfort in the injection areas.

Some people may experience mild redness and bruising on the affected area after a Botox treatment. In rare cases, there can be some asymmetry of the facial expressions due to differing absorption of the Botox into the muscles, or effects being felt in muscles that were not meant to be injected.

On the other hand, some patients have no visible side effects, especially when they comply with the clinic’s patient guidelines and instructions.

Botox is for people who aim to reduce the appearance of their facial wrinkles. It is also an excellent treatment for people who want to prevent the formation of fine lines as early as possible.

Patients in their 20s and 30s are great candidates for preventative Botox procedures. But patients as young as 18 years old may already start getting regular micro Botox doses.

Botulinum Toxin treatments are suitable for all skin types. However, if you have a relevant allergy or if you are unsure if there are any other concerns that might affect your suitability for the treatment, do consult our doctors.
