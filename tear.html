Tear Trough Filler

What is Tear Trough Filler?

A tear trough filler is also a type of under eye filler that tackles the issue of tired-looking eyes.

There are different classes of tear troughs:
  • Class I: Inner corner of the eye
  • Class II: Extended laterally
  • Class III: Extends all the way from inner to outer eye

The main purpose of the tear trough filler is to improve the appearance of dark eye circles that were caused by volume loss. Hence, the treatment will involve using an injectable under the eyes, adding volume beneath the eyelids, so that it will not look as sunken as before.

How does the treatment work?

Here’s what you may expect from the doctor during the treatment process:
  • i. Applying a topical anaesthetic cream or numbing cream
  • ii. Injecting with hyaluronic acid gel (helps to boost collagen production)
  • iii. Injecting of tear trough filler which also contains anaesthetic properties for additional comfort

Do note that alcohol consumption should be avoided 24 hours prior to the treatment.

The number of syringes needed may vary from one individual to another as for individuals with shallow tear troughs, one syringe may be used for both sides. While for individuals with deep tear troughs, one syringe per side may be used.


Since tear trough filler is a temporary treatment, we recommend our patients repeat the injection every 1-2 years for the ultimate effect.

Tear trough filler treatment may be a little uncomfortable but they are not painful. Many patients have shared that it wasn’t as painful as they expected, since a topical numbing cream will be applied before the start of the treatment.

Individuals of any age can undergo this treatment. We would recommend the treatment to individuals who have mild to moderate sagging under the eyes, have healthy and thick skin, and wish to have a more rejuvenated look.

Yes, it is possible to have other cosmetics treatments with tear trough fillers. You may approach our doctors to find out other treatments that are ideal for you
